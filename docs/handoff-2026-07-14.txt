Handoff, end of the cloud session, 2026-07-14.

Written for whoever picks this up locally, human or otherwise. Plain text on purpose. Everything described here is on the branch claude/full-audit-kqozzy, which is ahead of main and clean, all gates green at time of writing: 137 tests passing on normal and python -O runs, ruff clean, branch coverage 94 percent against a 90 floor. Sean will merge and fetch on his side; there is no open PR for this batch by his choice.

What this session was. It started as a full audit of Phase 1 and became five audit passes, a CI pipeline, and roughly two dozen fixes, plus four strategy documents. The shape of it: audit, fix, let a fresh set of eyes re-audit, fix what they find, repeat until the findings ran dry. They ran dry. The finding curve across the five passes went five confirmed defects, then four plus five, then five low and nit, then one low plus two nits, severity strictly falling, and the last pass could not break anything older than a day. The code has converged.

Where the record lives. Every audit is filed in docs, dated: full-audit-2026-07-13 (the A and B findings), audit-bite-4-web-2026-07-13 (the W findings against the reading room), audit-independent-opus-2026-07-14 (the F and N findings from the first independent auditor), and audit-final-loop-2026-07-14 (the S findings from the second). Each has an Outcome section saying what was fixed and where. Every finding that was fixed has a regression test, and most of those tests were written to fail against the pre-fix code, which was checked.

What actually got built beyond fixes. CI in .github/workflows/ci.yml: a test matrix across Python 3.11 through 3.13 on Ubuntu plus 3.11 and 3.13 on Windows, the whole suite re-run under -O (this caught two real assert-as-control bugs during the session, including one of mine), a pinned ruff gate, and a branch-coverage floor. The design and its per-phase growth plan are in docs/ci.md. Two policy test files carry the structural guarantees: tests/test_no_egress.py for the core, tests/test_boundaries.py for the whole package, the seam allowlist, the stdlib-only rule, and the loopback bind pin. The store gained a real transaction layer (Store.transaction, nestable, rolls back on failure including commit-time failure), the consent history is now in the tamper-evident chain, the chain head is anchored against tail truncation, and the web layer got its full hardening pass: CSRF token, Host allowlist against DNS rebinding, https-only card links, absence-and-garbage-never-graded, Content-Length defense, all socket-level behavior pinned by tests that drive a real loopback server.

The strategy documents, which are the part Sean should reread rather than skim: docs/early-stage-gaps-2026-07-14.md is the is-it-the-right-thing audit, six gaps ranked, and its number one outranks everything else in this handoff: no child has used this yet, and the machine is now more than ready to learn from one. docs/governmental-scale.md records the scale aspiration as a decision record; the load-bearing ideas there are the reporting seam (de-identified rollups computed on device, decided on paper so nobody ever just syncs the databases) and the rule that the zero-dependency auditable discipline is a product feature that outranks convenience.
