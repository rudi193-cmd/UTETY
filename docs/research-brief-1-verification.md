## CORRECTED by source

- **WCAG 2.2 new success criteria — the brief's self-flagged weak point, and it was right to
  flag it.** Per **W3C** (w3.org/WAI/.../new-in-22): WCAG 2.2 adds **9** criteria over 2.1 —
  **4 at Level AA** (2.4.11 Focus Not Obscured (Min); 2.5.7 Dragging Movements; 2.5.8 Target
  Size (Min); 3.3.8 Accessible Authentication (Min)), **2 at Level A** (3.2.6 Consistent Help;
  3.3.7 Redundant Entry), **3 at Level AAA** (2.4.12; 2.4.13 Focus Appearance; 3.3.9). Also,
  **4.1.1 Parsing is removed/obsolete** in 2.2.
  - The brief said "six new **Level AA**" and listed 3.2.6 and 3.3.7 *as AA* — **wrong level**;
    they are Level A. It also slipped 2.4.12 (AAA) into its "AA" list.
  - The defensible "6": to **conform at Level AA** you must meet all A + AA criteria, so **6 of
    the 9 new criteria are in-scope for AA conformance** (4 AA + 2 A); the 3 AAA are out. State
    it that way — it matters when writing a VPAT/ACR.
